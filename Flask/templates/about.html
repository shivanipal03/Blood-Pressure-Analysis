What is Blood Pressure?

Blood pressure (BP) measures the force of bllod purshing against artery walls. Maintaining healthy BP levels is crucial for overall cardiovascular health and preventing diseases like heart attackand stroke. It's measured in millimeters of mercury (mmHg) and recorded with two numbers:

  1. Systolic Pressure: The pressure when the heart beats while pumping blood.
  2. Diastolic Pressure: The pressure when the heart is at rest between beats.

Normal Blood Pressure Range

  1. Normal BP: Systolic < 120 mmHg and Diastolic < 80 mmHg
  2. Elevated BP: Systolic < 120-129 mmHg and Diastolic < 80 mmHg
  3. Hypertension Stage 1: Systolic < 130-139 mmHg or Diastolic < 80-89 mmHg
  4. Hypertension Stage 2: Systolic ≥ 140 mmHg or Diastolic ≥ 90 mmHg

Causes of High Blood Pressure

  1. Primary(Essential) Hypertension: No identifiable cause, develops over many years.
  2. Secondary Hypertension: Caused by an underlying condition, such as kidney disease, adrenal gland tumors, thyroid problems, or certain medications.

Symptoms of High Blood Pressure

Often called the "silent killer" because it may not cause symptoms. When symptoms do occur, they can include:

  1. Headaches
  2. Shortness of breath
  3. Nosebleeds

Complications

  1. Heart Attack or Stroke: High BP cancause hardening and thickening of the arteries.
  2. Aneurysm: Increased BP can cause blood vessels to weaken and bulge.
  3. Heart Failure: The heart has to work harder to pump blood.
  4. Kidney Damage: Can lead to kidney failure.
